<td><h5 id="binder-method-of-barcode-label-storage">Binder Method of Barcode Label Storage</h5></td>
<td><blockquote>
<p>Milwaukee uses three-ring binders with photo holders that contain four pockets per page to hold barcode labels for different items. Since their inventory rooms are far away from the Purchasing Agent’s location, they bring the labels into the office and organize them into binders. This is one method of utilizing the barcode labels.</p>
<p>Each binder can correlate to a Location in your inventory. For instance, you can have a binder for Orthotic Lab, Wheelchairs, satellite patient outpatient clinic, Room C-26, etc.</p>
<p>You can include a Table of Contents for each binder of items listed alphabetically. For instance, you might want a category for Ambulatory and list the items:</p>
</blockquote>
<ul>
<li><p>Cane – Blind #33</p></li>
<li><p>Cane – Pistol Grip</p></li>
<li><p>Cane – Wood – Tall</p></li>
</ul>
<blockquote>
<p>Each item would also be typed onto a label that would be affixed to a pocket on the plastic photo holders. As items are received into inventory and the barcode labels are printed, you can stuff the sheet of labels into these pockets.</p>
</blockquote></td>

<td><h5 id="use-the-oldest-labels-first">Use the oldest labels first</h5></td>
<td><blockquote>
<p>Pay careful attention to the date on the barcode labels. Use the oldest label first when scanning. Make sure that the inventory being assigned correlates to the barcode label date. The oldest item and oldest barcode label should be used first.</p>
<p>If a problem exists with scanning a label when doing a Stock Issue, check to make sure that the oldest label (for the oldest stock item) is being used first. If you have multiple Purchasing Agents ordering the same item for different patients from the same inventory location, you may want to check that the oldest item is being entered first. This may take coordinated communication.</p>
<p><strong>Note:</strong> Also make sure the item hasn’t been transferred to a different location after the label was printed.</p>
</blockquote></td>

<td><h5 id="barcode-labels-are-not-scanning">Barcode Labels are not scanning</h5></td>
<td><blockquote>
<p>Milwaukee reported that while scanning a barcode label, the following message displayed <strong>“* The Item scanned is not available, please update your inventory!!!”</strong> This message appears even though the item is physically on the shelf.</p>
<p>Prosthetics users should double-check their inventory as to why the label is not being accepted during scanning. One of the possible causes of this message is because the item was transferred. The user may think that the item(s) are still in inventory, but in reality, it’s already gone. Depending on what the user did to that particular item (batch) - transferred, deactivated the location where this item resides, edited the quantity or deletion of a Stock Issue, the label that was printed may not work. This is because our inventory system is date sensitive.</p>
<p>Be careful of the following options when scanning a barcode label:</p>
</blockquote>
<ul>
<li><p>From the <strong>Stock Issues (SI)</strong> menu: <u><strong>Edit/Delete Issue From Stock (ED)</strong> option</u>.</p></li>
<li><p>From the <strong>Prosthetics Main Menu</strong>: <u><strong>Edit 2319 (ED2)</strong> option</u></p></li>
<li><p>From the <strong>NPPD Tools</strong> menu: <strong>Quick Edit 2319 Record (QED2)</strong> option</p></li>
</ul></td>
</tr>

<td><blockquote>
<p>Milwaukee had IRM temporarily put a supervisory lock on the <strong>Edit/Delete Issue from Stock</strong> <strong>(ED)</strong> option (from the <strong>Purchasing (PU)</strong> Menu). That way, the Supervisor will know when the PA needs to use this option to return an item, and they can manually make the adjustments in PIP.</p>

<td><h5 id="physical-count">Physical Count</h5></td>
<td><blockquote>
<p>It is helpful to do a physical count periodically at least monthly during testing. Use the <strong>Print Stock Work Sheet (WS)</strong> report for the physical count. This report provides the quantity of the item in stock as well as a blank column for the physical count for each item.</p>
<p><strong><u>Recommendations for Testing</u>:</strong> It is recommended that a physical count be done monthly for the most active HCPCS codes. Once your system is Live with this patch, then it is also recommended to do an entire physical count quarterly for your site. If a problem arises, it is always helpful to conduct the physical count at that point in time then to wait quarterly, especially on an active HCPCS code.</p>
</blockquote></td>

<td><h5 id="barcode-label-printing-tips">Barcode label printing tips</h5></td>
<td><blockquote>
<p>If you use the new option, <strong>Print All Barcode in a Location (AL)</strong>, your printer may skip or overprint a label at times. Verify the labels for all information in case a barcode or just a description is printed. You can use the <strong>Reprint Barcode Label (BC)</strong> option to print a second label.</p>
<p>If the labels are not put exactly in the printer properly, the printer can start printing in the middle of the label cutting off the item’s description and or printing on two labels - either one unusable. Also, the labels can get wrapped in the printer roller causing a label jam. <strong>To be sure the barcode printer is ready for printing, do a FORM FEED.</strong> This way the start of the printout will always be at the top of the label.</p>
<p><strong>Note:</strong> Sometimes, at the end of the barcode label, all the digits for the date are not printed due to limited space. With this patch, it now prints the last two digits of the year - instead of 12/23/2002, it prints 12/23/02.</p>
</blockquote></td>
