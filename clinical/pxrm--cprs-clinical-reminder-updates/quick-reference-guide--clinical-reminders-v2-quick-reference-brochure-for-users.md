<td><h6 id="section"></h6>
<p>Benefits of Reminders</p>
<p>“Reminders help us to do the right thing consistently well” – Dr. Jonathan Perlin</p>
<p>What are clinical reminders and what</p>
<p>do they do for clinicians?</p>
<ul>
<li><blockquote>
<p>Inform clinicians when a patient is due to receive clinical activity.</p>
</blockquote></li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li><blockquote>
<p>Target the clinicians who can manage and resolve the clinical activity most appropriately.</p>
</blockquote></li>
<li><blockquote>
<p>Identify patients to whom a reminder applies, based on <strong>V</strong><em>IST</em><strong>A</strong> patient data.</p>
</blockquote></li>
<li><blockquote>
<p>Identify the clinical activities that resolve or satisfy reminders.</p>
</blockquote></li>
<li><blockquote>
<p>Summarize pertinent patient information to help clinicians determine appropriate follow-up activities.</p>
</blockquote></li>
<li><blockquote>
<p>Allow clinicians to resolve reminders through CPRS.</p>
</blockquote></li>
<li><blockquote>
<p>Provide aggregate reports that assist clinicians in managing their entire patient caseload.</p>
</blockquote></li>
<li><blockquote>
<p>Support national clinical practice guidelines.</p>
</blockquote></li>
</ul></td>

<td><p><strong>Resolving Reminders</strong></p>
<p>1. Log on to CPRS and select the <em>Notes</em> tab.</p>
<p>2. Click on the <strong>New Note</strong> button.</p>
<p>3. Select any title or type a new title in the title box.</p>
<p>4. Choose a visit with which to associate the note. You can use the TELEPHONE CLINIC selection for telephone notes.</p>
<p>5. When the note opens, "drawers" open in the lower left section of the screen. Click on the <strong>Reminders</strong> drawer to open it.</p>
<p>![](clinical-reminders-version-2-quick-reference-brochure-for-users/010.png)6. When the Reminders drawer opens, you can see the reminders on which you can act.  </p>
<p>7. Click on a reminder to select it for resolution. A reminder dialog opens. You can resolve one or several reminders in a single note. As you select items in the dialog by clicking on the checkboxes, Clinical Reminders enters text into the progress note and may also automatically write orders for labs, consults, etc. You can click on a selected item to undo the selection.</p>
<p>![](clinical-reminders-version-2-quick-reference-brochure-for-users/011.png)</p>
<p>Notice that selections in the dialog have corresponding text in the pane below the dialog. You can edit, add to, or delete any of this text just as you would text in any progress note.</p></td>

<p>Identify patients in a panel or a clinic who have reminders that are due</p>

<p>Identify inpatients who have reminders that are due</p>
</blockquote></li>
<li><blockquote>
<p>Identify patients who will be in a specific clinic in the next month and who need an intervention</p>
</blockquote></li>
<li><blockquote>
<p>Identify patients who have left a specific clinic in the past week and who missed having an intervention</p>
</blockquote></li>
<li><blockquote>
<p>Identify patients who have lab findings but no diagnoses</p>
</blockquote></li>
<li><blockquote>
<p>Track employee health</p>
</blockquote></li>
<li><blockquote>
<p>Obtain information for inpatient education, exams, and immunizations</p>
</blockquote></li>
<li><blockquote>
<p>Report on patients who are eligible for a study</p>
</blockquote></li>
<li><blockquote>
<p>Identify high-risk patients</p>
</blockquote></li>
<li><blockquote>
<p>Report on disease-specific reminders: HCV, HIV, Melanoma, AAA, Diabetes</p>
</blockquote></li>
</ul>
<blockquote>
<p><strong>Health Summary &amp; Reminders</strong></p>
<p>You can define health summaries with three different reminder components and you can define each of these components with selected reminders. The reminders you select for each health summary should target the clinic or clinicians best suited for clinical intervention when the reminders are due. When you run a health summary, Clinical Reminders evaluates the patient’s data and returns the results to the health summary for display. The format of the display is based on the component defined in the health summary.</p>
<p>Clinical Reminders 2 contains new health summary components to support the <strong>My Health<em>e</em>Vet</strong> project. These components contain less technical information than in most health summary components, to provide a more suitable display of clinical reminder information to patients.</p>
</blockquote></td>
